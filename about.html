About Noah

Noah Satterlee was a 12 year old boy diagnosed with a primary immunodeficiency among many other things whose illnesses were causing him to miss his 6th grade year. It was also the year for him to begin studying for his Bar Mitzvah and thinking of a mitzvah (good deed) project. Noah had been in and out of the hospital many, many times, not just locally, but also sent out of state. He had seen his parents struggling to get supplies together last minute or having to leave him to get necessary things like a toothbrush or shampoo. Noah also realized that not everyone has two parents, one to stay at the hospital and one to get needed items, nor does everyone have the time before being rushed to a hospital. He also saw the stress that his parents were under trying to care for him. This is when the idea for Noah’s Care Bags was born. Noah wanted to put together a little bag with travel sized toiletries, some candy (because chocolate makes everything better), and a message letting families know that they aren’t alone with their struggle. Noah had learned that even though he has his struggles, that there are others who may be struggling more. He realized that he can make a difference in this world even though he is “just a kid.” One of his favorite quotes is “Stars can’t shine without darkness.” Noah asked family and friends if they had toiletries to donate so he could make 100 bags to donate to Upstate Golisano Children’s Hospital in Syracuse NY. He collected enough for almost 200 bags! That's when he decided he wanted to keep collecting and donating. Noah’s Care Bags has helped over 1,000 families in three states so far. (Check out our map showing where we have donated!) Any items donated that don’t fit in the bags were donated to the Ronald McDonald House to help families that have to stay close to their child.
